The Marvelous One

Marvin Hagler - Undisputed Middle Weight Champion 1980 - 1987

Marvin Hagler is undoubtedly one of the greatest boxers of all time, reigning supreme as undisputed middle weight champion from 1980 to 1987.He made twelve successful title defenses in that period, all by knockout save one. He was one of the luminaries in the much lauded late 70's to early 80's "era of the four kings" (Roberto Duran, Marvelous Marvin Hagler, Sugar Ray Leanard, Thomas "Hitman" Hearns).

Career Highlights

  • 1969 Amature Career -Hagler took up boxing at the age of 15 after being beaten up on the street by a local boxer, whom he later defeated. He finished his amatuer career with a 55-1 record.

  • 1973 Turned Pro -Hagler was a top-ranked middleweight for several years before he fought for the title. He often struggled to find high-profile opponents to face him.

  • 1979 First Title Shot -Hagler fought middleweight champion Vito Antuofermo to a controversial draw after fifteen rounds. He would avenge this result in a later fight with a TKO of Antuofermo in the fourth round.

  • 1980 World Champion -Hagler defeats british boxer Alan Minter in brutal fashion in a three round stoppage to become world middleweight champion.

  • 1983 Hagler vs. Duran -Hagler won a unanimous decision against the great Roberto Duran after fifteen rounds.

  • 1985 Hagler vs. Hearns -In a fight that was later dubbed "The War", Hagler defeats "Hitman" Hearns in three brutal rounds by TKO. The fight only lasted eight minutes and one second, but was named "Fight of the Year" by The Ring magazine, and is still considered a classic to this day.

  • 1986 Hagler vs. Mugabi -Hagler stopped Olympic silver medalist John Mugabi of Uganda in the eleventh round of a blistering battle. Mugabi came into this fight 26-0 with 26 knockouts and was ranked the top contender by all three major sanctioning bodies.

  • 1987 Hagler vs. Leonard -Hagler's last fight as a professional was a controversial 12-round loss to Sugar Ray Leonard. Hagler later said that as the fighters embraced at the end of the bout, Leonard told him, "You beat me". Leonard denied making the statement, but HBO cameras and microphones supported Hagler's version of events.
